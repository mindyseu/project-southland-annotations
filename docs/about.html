more* is a two-day workshop. An asterisk gives us more. It adds layers of information to a primary text. It encodes expletives, repairs mistakes, and inserts personal voice. It is an additive gesture. In this Southland Institute workshop, we will co-author and distribute a group publication that amplifies the potential of this glyph. The online publication is multidimensional.link.

Day 1

  1. Let’s introduce ourselves. Using this collaborative drawing tool, draw a line and add your name. Laura and Mindy will begin.
  2. In this lecture, Laura and Mindy will introduce the asterisk in its many forms.
  3. Individual Exercise: Make your own asterisk
    • Using any tool, create an asterisk.
    • Consider the tool. Are you drawing this in Illustrator or Photoshop? Writing it with pen and paper? Photographing a clothing sculpture?
    • Consider the form. How many arms does it have? What are different ways of creating a star?
    • Consider the function. How does it operate?
    • No matter how you create your asterisk, download this artboard in Illustrator and make the asterisk into an .svg
    • Your SVG should be exported as a black vector.
    • Select a color and add it next to your name in this spreadsheet.
  4. Group Exercise: Create a collective, expanded bibliography
    • Using books around you, select an excerpt about... (character count)
    • Add a photograph of the excerpt to this Are.na channel.Include the book citation in the description.
    • Add a transcription of the excerpt to this g-doc.
  5. Feel free to work on your excerpt or asterisk throughout the week. Next Saturday, we will utilize the asterisks in our group text.

Day 2

  1. We’ve added your names and usernames to this spreadsheet.
  2. With the username we’ve assigned, create a free account on Hypothes.is.
  3. Please visit multidimensional.link.
  4. Using the “edit” link in the bottom-left, your Hypothes.is account will allow you to annotate the base layer we wrote last week. When you click “view”, you’ll see that your name has been added to the side panel and your annotation appears inline with your asterisk.
  5. For the next hour, we will spend 20 minutes each on the three prompts below:
    • Amend the base layer with text.
    • Censor the base layer with an image.
    • xxx the base layer with audio.
  6. Each participant gives a 2-minute annotation tour on multidimensional.link/southland.
